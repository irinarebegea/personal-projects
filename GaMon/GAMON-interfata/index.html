Get rid of illegal dumps and recycle more

The first step in cleaning up the world is knowing where all the “dirty spots” are. Use GaMon to report any illegal dump you come across. Environmental organisations and municipalities in the area will be notified.

Why should you care?

Each person generates on average 1 kilogram of waste per day. That's enough waste to fill 285 000 trucks.

Part of it gets recycled but a huge amount of trash ends up on illegal dumps. These dumps are everywhere - in our cities, nature, rivers and oceans.

GaMon is an environmental project which aims to map all illegal dumps and help citizens recycle more.

How does it work?

What happens after you report an illegal dump


1. You report an illegal dump

You found an illegal dump, took a picture and reported it using GaMon.


2. Relevant people get notified

Environmental organisations and municipalities will be notified.


3. Cleaning event is organized

Environmental organisation or municipality organises a cleaning event. You can even join in.


4. Dump you've reported is cleaned up
